### OneLiner

Point mutation leading to a single amino acid replacement in the Î²-chain of hemoglobin causing hemolysis  describes sickle cell anemia, which presents during childhood with various complications such as hematopoiesis into the skull and facial bones, extramedullary hematopoiesis with hepatomegaly, dactylitis (swollen feet and hands), autosplenectomy, pain crisis, renal papillary necrosis, or acute chest syndrome.
